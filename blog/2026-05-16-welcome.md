This is the first post on a blog I should have started months ago.

`narenana.com` is where I build small, browser-native tools for RC pilots
— starting with the [RC Log Viewer](/log-viewer/), a 3D replay of
EdgeTX, iNAV, and Betaflight flight logs. No upload, no install, runs
entirely on your machine.

## Why a blog

There are a handful of things I keep wanting to write up — usually
after a long debug session, or after someone in a Discord thread
asks the same question for the third time.

A few examples queued up:

- **"View iNAV blackbox in your browser — no install"** — how the
  Rust → WASM parser works, the two-tier fallback with the C parser,
  and why the iNAV 9 enum rollover broke everything for two weeks.
- **"What EdgeTX captures in a CSV — and what it doesn't"** — the
  fields, the units, the assumptions baked into the OpenTX-era
  schema, and why telemetry resolution affects what the viewer can
  reconstruct.
- **"How the flicker turned out to be browser rAF cadence"** — the
  multi-day investigation behind the camera-director release, with
  the diagnostic probes that finally found it.
- **"Why offline-first matters for flight log analysis"** — the
  privacy case, the build-time case, and the deployment case.

If any of those would be useful to you in a particular order, the
GitHub issue tracker at
[narenana/edgetx-log-parser](https://github.com/narenana/edgetx-log-parser)
is the right place to ask.

## What's actually here

Right now: this post and the [log viewer](/log-viewer/). The viewer
parses two formats — EdgeTX/OpenTX CSV from your transmitter SD card,
and iNAV / Betaflight blackbox binaries from your flight controller —
and renders a 3D flyby with synced telemetry charts. It's open source
under GPL-3.0 and ships as a PWA, so it works offline once you've
loaded it.

If you find a log that doesn't parse, that's a bug I want to know
about. Open an issue with the file (you can scrub the GPS coords if
you want; the parser doesn't care).
